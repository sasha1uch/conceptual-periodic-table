Explanation of Organization

Groups (Columns)
Each column represents a family of technology systems with similar properties. Hardware manufacturers such as Apple, Nvidia, and Samsung behave similarly because they build physical infrastructure. Hybrid companies like Microsoft or Amazon combine hardware systems with large-scale software services. Digital platforms such as Netflix or Spotify primarily exist as software ecosystems.

Periods (Rows)
Rows represent increasing technological influence and scale. Companies lower in the table represent larger infrastructure or broader technological impact globally.

Categories
Metals represent hardware systems and physical technology. Metalloids represent hybrid companies that connect hardware and software ecosystems. Nonmetals represent digital platforms and software services.

Atomic Radius Trend
In this conceptual system radius represents technological influence. Influence increases down the table because companies lower in the system operate at larger global scale.

Ionization Energy Trend
Ionization energy represents how difficult a company would be to replace within the technology ecosystem. It increases across the table from left to right because platforms become more unique and harder to substitute.

Why the System Works
This structure mirrors the real periodic table by organizing items into families, arranging rows by increasing magnitude, separating categories, and demonstrating two opposing trends similar to atomic radius and ionization energy.
