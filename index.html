Required Explanation Notes

1. How I organized my groups and periods

The groups are organized by shared technological properties. Group 1 contains hardware and device companies, Group 2 contains hybrid software and cloud systems, Group 3 contains digital media and internet platforms, and Group 4 contains specialized high-impact tech systems. The periods show increasing influence and ecosystem size as you move downward.

2. How I represented metals, nonmetals, and metalloids

Metals represent physical technology and hardware-oriented companies. Metalloids represent companies that combine traits from both sides by connecting software, cloud systems, and infrastructure. Nonmetals represent digital platforms and online services that are less physical and more service-based.

3. How radius trends in my table

Radius represents technological influence or ecosystem size. It increases down a group, so companies lower in the same column have a larger conceptual radius. It decreases across a period from left to right, so items at the left side of a row have the broadest radius within that row.

4. How ionization energy trends in my table

Ionization energy represents how hard a company or platform is to replace. It increases across a period from left to right, so companies on the right are harder to remove from the system. It decreases down a group, which makes it opposite to the radius trend just like in the real periodic table.

5. Why my system makes sense

This system makes sense because it mirrors the logic of the real periodic table instead of copying real chemistry facts. The columns act like families because the companies in each group share similar roles. The rows act like periods because they show a steady increase in influence. The three broad categories mirror metals, metalloids, and nonmetals, and the table also includes two opposite trends: radius and ionization energy. That shows I understand how the periodic table is structured even though the theme is completely conceptual.
