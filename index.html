Conceptual Organization

Groups (Columns)
Companies in the same column share similar technological roles. Hardware companies form one family, hybrid infrastructure companies form another, and digital platforms form a third. This mirrors how elements in the periodic table share chemical properties within groups.

Periods (Rows)
Rows represent increasing technological scale and global impact. Companies higher in the table operate on smaller ecosystems, while those lower in the table influence larger global systems.

Categories
Metals represent hardware‑focused technology. Metalloids represent hybrid systems combining hardware and software infrastructure. Nonmetals represent digital platforms and online services.

Radius Trend
Radius represents technological influence or ecosystem size. Influence increases down the table as companies operate at larger global scales.

Ionization Energy Trend
Ionization energy represents how difficult a technology platform would be to replace. It increases across the table from left to right as systems become more unique and specialized.

Why the System Works
The system mirrors the logic of the periodic table by organizing companies into families with shared properties, arranging rows by increasing magnitude, separating broad categories, and demonstrating opposing periodic trends.
