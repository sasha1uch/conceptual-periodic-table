Required Explanation Notes

1. How I organized my groups and periods

The groups are based on shared roles. Group 1 is hardware and chips, Group 2 is software and cloud, Group 3 is internet and streaming platforms, and Group 4 is specialized tech with a big impact. The periods show increasing influence as you move down.

2. How I represented metals, nonmetals, and metalloids

Metals are the companies tied most closely to physical technology. Metalloids are the ones in between, since they connect software and infrastructure. Nonmetals are mostly platform-based services that live online.

3. How radius trends in my table

Radius means influence or ecosystem size. It increases down a group, so the companies lower in a column have a larger conceptual radius. It decreases from left to right across a row.

4. How ionization energy trends in my table

Ionization energy means how hard something is to replace. It increases across a row from left to right, so the companies on the right are harder to remove. It decreases down a group, which makes it the opposite of radius.

5. Why my system makes sense

This system works because it follows the same logic as a real periodic table. The columns act like families, the rows show steady change, the colors separate broad categories, and the two trends move in opposite directions. Even though the theme is tech companies, the structure still shows that I understand how a periodic table is built.
